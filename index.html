A timeline of Kentaro Miura's life:

  • 1966 - Born in Chiba, Chiba Prefecture, Japan.

  • 1976 - Created his first manga, which was published for his classmates in a school publication.

  • 1977 - Created his second manga in which he used India Ink for the first time.

  • 1979 - In middle school he starts using professional drawing techniques.

  • 1982 - In High School, Miura enrolls in an artistic curriculum. He and his classmates publish their work in school booklets. He briefly works as an assistant to George Morikawa who dismisses him because he acknowledges that there is nothing he could teach him.

  • 1985 - Miura applies for entrance to the art college of Nihon University. He is granted admission.

  • 1988 - He published a prototype of Berserk in Hakusensha's Monthly Comicom, the 48-page prototype placed second in ComiComi's 7th Manga School competition.

  • 1989 - The full serialization of Berserk would begin. Berzerk is Miura's most successful and famous work.

  • 1997 - Miura supervised the production of a 25-episode anime adaptation of Berserk produced by OLM, Inc., which aired in the same year on NTV. He also supervised the 1999 Dreamcast video gameSword of the Guts' Rage.

  • 2002 - Miura received the Award for Excellence at the 6th Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prizes for Berserk.

  • 2006 - Berserk started to go on frequent and often extended hiatuses, and alternated between monthly and irregular serialization.

  • 2021 - Miura died due to acute aortic dissection, at the age of 54. His death was publicly announced on May 20, 2021. A private ceremony was held by his family. Berserk had more than 50 million copies in circulation worldwide, including digital versions. The series also spawned a host of merchandise, both official and fan-made, ranging from statues, action figures to key rings, video games, and a trading card game.

Legacy

Established as one of the best-selling manga of all time, Miura's series Berserk impacted the manga medium and beyond, with journalist Jade King stating: "[It] is difficult to overstate the tremendous impact his work has had on the world of games, manga, film, anime, and even literature." The image of Guts and his massive sword is attributed to inspiring characters like Cloud Strife of Final Fantasy VII and Dante from the Devil May Cry series, with the overall aesthetic of Berserk inspiring the monsters and world of the Dark Souls series. Video game director Hideaki Itsuno and producer Hiroyuki Kobayashi are fans of Berserk, and the role-playing hack and slash game Dragon's Dogma included armor based on Guts and Griffith's. During a GDC talk in 2019, Itsuno stated that the tone and style of Devil May Cry 5 was inspired from Berzerk.
